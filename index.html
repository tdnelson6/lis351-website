Why Visit Kalamazoo?

Rich History

Founded in 1831, Kalamazoo became known for celery farming, paper production, and early automobile manufacturing.

Outdoor Recreation

Explore the Kalamazoo River Valley Trail, scenic parks, and kayaking adventures along the river.

Arts & Culture

Enjoy the Kalamazoo Institute of Arts, local festivals, live music, and one of the first outdoor pedestrian malls in the U.S.

Featured Attraction: Air Zoo Aerospace & Science Museum

The Kalamazoo Air Zoo is one of the city's most popular destinations, offering interactive exhibits, historic aircraft, flight simulators, and educational programs for visitors of all ages. It showcases Kalamazoo's connection to aviation and innovation.

Education

Western Michigan University and Kalamazoo College bring strong academic programs and vibrant student life to the city.

Events & Culture

From the Kalamazoo Blues Festival to Art Hop and farmers markets, the city offers year-round community events and entertainment.
