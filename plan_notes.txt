    About Kalamazoo	
        Kalamazoo is located in Southwest Michigan and is home to Western Michigan University. 
        Founded in 1831, the city has grown into a cultural and educational hub known for innovation, 
        healthcare, and manufacturing.	
        
        downtown_kalamazoo.jpg

    History of Kalamazoo 
        became famous in the 19th century for celery farming and later for its paper production industry. 
        The city also played a role in early automobile manufacturing.	
        
        kalamazoo_historic.jpg

    Top Attractions	
        Popular attractions include the Kalamazoo Air Zoo Aerospace & Science Museum, Kalamazoo Institute 
        of Arts, Kalamazoo Mall (one of the first outdoor pedestrian malls in the U.S.), and Bell’s Brewery.	
        
        air_zoo.jpg

    Outdoor Activities	
        Visitors can enjoy hiking and biking on the Kalamazoo River Valley Trail, exploring local parks, 
        and kayaking along the Kalamazoo River.	
        
        kalamazoo_trail.jpg

    Education	
        Western Michigan University and Kalamazoo College bring a strong academic presence and student 
        life to the city. 
        
        wmu_campus.jpg

    Events & Culture	
        Kalamazoo hosts events such as the Kalamazoo Blues Festival, Art Hop, and local farmers markets, 
        offering a lively arts and music scene year-round.
